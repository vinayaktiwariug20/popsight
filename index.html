AI sourcing copilot for product discovery and market intelligence

PopSight is a full-stack consumer packaged goods sourcing workspace that helps teams discover trending products, analyze market signals, and chat with a grounded copilot over catalog, vendor, inventory, and sales-history data. I focused on the memory and retrieval pieces inside the conversational copilot module for the winning hackathon build.

Overview

What it does

PopSight helps sourcing teams discover products, surface trends, and ask questions over grounded company data. The system combines retrieval, memory, and tool use so the assistant can respond with context instead of generic LLM output.

Outcome

The project won the Prince of Peace AI Product Discovery & Trend Intelligence track at Hack the Coast 2026.

My focus

I focused on the conversational copilot’s memory and retrieval behavior: durable memory controls, current-session context, and RAG over internal company data.

My Role

Owned area: conversational sourcing copilot

  • Built the memory CRUD behavior for durable long-term memory entries and conversational memory state.
  • Worked on the LangGraph-based multi-tool agent for current scan context, product lookup, recent-conversation search, memory save/search/delete, and Qdrant retrieval.
  • Implemented the memory layer used for short-term session state and long-term sourcing preferences, supplier notes, and strategic decisions.
  • Built the ingestion workflow for catalog, vendor, inventory, and sales-history documents into Qdrant.
  • Added deterministic demo fallbacks so the app remained usable during hackathon judging even when rate limits or missing keys caused failures.
  • Presented the project during judging and answered the higher-level product and deployment questions from sponsors and evaluators.

What the rest of the team handled

  • Product fetch and compliance signal extraction.
  • Trend interpretation and product discovery graph.
  • Shared full-stack integration and demo coordination.

This separation keeps the page accurate and avoids claiming ownership of the full system.

Architecture

Backend

FastAPI service exposing scanning, chat, memory, and retrieval endpoints.

Knowledge Layer

Qdrant vector retrieval over chunked company documents, plus structured metadata in PostgreSQL.

Agent Layer

LangGraph orchestration with tool calling, session state, and long-term memory.
